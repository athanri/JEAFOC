About Celtic Biotech

Celtic Biotech is an Irish bio-pharmaceutical company developing novel peptides for the treatment of solid tumours, with a specific focus on non-small cell lung cancer. With current treatments often falling short in efficacy, safety, and patient convenience, the Company has identified promising naturally derived receptor binding proteins, one of which, CB-24 crotoxin, it has progressed into Phase I trials using a novel intra-patient dose escalating protocol.

The drug has shown excellent safety, minimal side-effects and stabilisation of disease in several patients in Phase Ib (6 patients evaluated) multi-cancer trials. Delivered as a user friendly at-home monotherapy using infusion pumps, the patients are monitored remotely requiring visits to clinic twice weekly for dose escalation. Part funded by the European Innovation Council, Phase Ic clinical trials will commence in 2024 to identify MTD and optimum treatment protocol, with a view to commencing Phase II trials in NSCLC 2025/26, in this potentially game-changing cancer therapeutic.

About Breakthrough Cancer Research

Breakthrough Cancer Research is an Irish medical research charity focused on cancer. We work to significantly impact the number of people who can survive this disease. We invest in world-class research to impact patients' quality of life and save lives. We are particularly focused on improving outcomes for those cancers, often called poor prognosis or low survival cancers, which are poorly served by current treatment options. In 2023 we launched our new research strategy (‘Making More Survivors’), building on immense success already delivered, sharpening our focus to channel even greater investment into seven specific low survival cancers, which are responsible for nearly half of all cancer deaths annually in Ireland and worldwide. Namely, Brain, Oesophageal, Lung, Liver, Stomach, Pancreatic and Ovarian cancers.

Breakthrough drives progress through innovative research programmes aimed at transforming the treatment and care for these cancers to deliver our bold vision: 100% survival for 100% of cancers.

About AICRIStart

The AICRIstart programme is a Foundation Stone for the All-Island Cancer Research Institute (AICRI) and will build critical mass in Precision Cancer Medicine on the island of Ireland. In March 2022, the programme was awarded €4 million in funding under the HEA North South Research Programme.

Ten academic institutions (UCD, TCD, RCSI, TU Dublin, DCU, QUB, UU, NUIG, UL and UCC) are involved in the project and UCD is the administrative lead.

AICRIstart is an all-island doctoral and post-doctoral research training programme, which will create a unique network of excellence in cancer research, as well as an exceptional training environment for emerging researchers.

About Ireland American Science Forum

The Ireland America Science Forum (IASF) was created in 2019 to strengthen existing and build new connections among scientists in North America and Northern Ireland and the Republic of Ireland.
